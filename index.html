MARSHAL OF THE INDIAN AIR FORCE LT.ARJAN SINGH

"The army took a few days. Always, the movement of the army is more difficult; the air force is much easier."

Marshal of the Indian Air Force Arjan Singh with the ceremonial baton.

    Here's a time line of Lt.Arjan singh:
  • Born on 14 Apr 1919 in Lyallpur, Punjab (now known as Faisalabad, Pakistan)
  • Arjan Singh was the fourth generation of his family to join the British Indian armed forces
  • Singh was educated at Montgomery, British India (now in Pakistan) and later entered the RAF College Cranwell in 1938
  • He was commissioned as a pilot officer in December 1939
  • Singh led No. 1 Squadron, Indian Air Force into combat during the Arakan Campaign in 1944
  • Singh was Chief of the Air Staff (CAS), from 1 August 1964 to 15 July 1969
  • Singh was awarded the Padma Vibhushan in 1965
  • He took retirement from his services in 1970 at the age of 50
  • In 1971, after his retirement, Singh was appointed as the Indian Ambassador to Switzerland and Vatican serving concurrently.
  • He was the Lt. Governor of Delhi from December 1989 to December 1990
  • Singh was made Marshal of the Air Force in January, 2002
  • Singh suffered a cardiac arrest at his New Delhi residence in the early morning of 16 September 2017 . He died at 7:47 p.m. (IST) that evening.
